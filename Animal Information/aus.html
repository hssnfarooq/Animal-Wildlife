Australia

Australia is home to a fascinating variety of species, including some of the rarest organisms on the planet. Australia's continent has large expanses of desert and semi-arid land in its interior, subtropical rain forest in the northeast, and grasslands and mountain ranges along its periphery. The kangaroo, koala, echidna, dingo, platypus, wallaby, and wombat are among the 90 percent of Australia's wild species that are present nowhere else. Australia is the world's smallest continent and is sometimes known as the world's biggest island. This separation has resulted in an ecosystem unlike any other.

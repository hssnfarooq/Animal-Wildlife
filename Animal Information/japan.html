Japan

Shaped by millions of years of volcanic activity, river flooding and tectonic shifts, the archipelago of Japan encompasses a diverse range of climates, from the icy north of Hokkaido to the sub-tropical southern islands. Because of this, the diverse ecosystems have become much more common and now include significant populations of different plant and animal species, which are not often found in the same geographic location. It's possible you never would have considered visiting Japan for safari, but the opportunity is certainly there, and you'll find some of the most magnificent animals in the world.
